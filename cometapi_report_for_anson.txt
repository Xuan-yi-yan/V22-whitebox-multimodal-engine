Here's my technical feedback from the test credits — I ran a cross-model dual-agent
debate benchmark on CometAPI, pitting Claude Opus 4.5 against Gemini 2.5 Pro on a

=== What I tested ===

Dual-model debate (4 rounds): Claude Opus 4.5 (A-side, architecture defense) vs
Gemini 2.5 Pro (B-side, critical review). Both models were fed the full V22 white-box
multimodal engine architecture document (~5,000 chars) and asked to debate its design
merits — covering character embedding, P3 attribute stack, ABCDEF cognitive stack,
memory read-write head, and spherical normalization.

=== Key findings ===

1. ROUTING STABILITY: Both Claude and Gemini completed their rounds without
   disconnections or errors. Latency was consistent (Claude ~44s per deep-analysis
   round, Gemini ~19s per review round).

2. CROSS-MODEL INTELLIGENCE: The debate revealed genuinely independent reasoning.
   Claude provided a detailed critique of the V22 architecture (parameter efficiency
   paradox, cascade error risk, training stability concerns). Gemini then independently
   DEFENDED V22 against Claude's criticisms — calling out inaccuracies in Claude's
   assumptions about P3 rule engine coverage, CharEmbed dimensionality, and Stage B
   parameter efficiency. This level of independent cross-model reasoning is remarkable
   and speaks to the quality of both the models and CometAPI's routing layer.

3. MODEL BEHAVIOR OBSERVATION: Gemini 2.5 Pro demonstrated stronger "first-principles"
   reasoning on white-box architecture design than Claude Opus 4.5 on this specific
   task — it correctly identified that V22's small-parameter design is an intentional
   information bottleneck, not a limitation. This kind of model-specific behavior
   insight is valuable for teams choosing between providers.

=== What this means for CometAPI ===

This benchmark demonstrates that CometAPI's routing layer can reliably host complex,
multi-round, cross-model reasoning tasks — the kind that AI research teams and
infrastructure engineers actually run, not just simple chat completions. The stability
across both Anthropic and Google models on the same platform is a genuine selling point.
